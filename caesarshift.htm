How it works

The Caesar Shift Cipher generates ciphertext characters by simply substituting the character that is "shift" places along the alphabet from the original plain-text character's position. For example, a shift of 2 would encode plaintext "a" as ciphertext "C" (using the convention of plain-text being lowercase and cipher-text uppercase). To get back to the original message, the receiver simply finds the plain-text character that is shift places further back than the cipher-text character in the plain-text alphabet. Since there are only 25 possible cipher-text alphabets, this cipher is trivial to break.
