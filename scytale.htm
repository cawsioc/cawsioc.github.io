How it works

A Scytale provides a simple means to transpose the letters of a plain-text message. A leather strap or similar medium is wound around the Scytale by the sender, and then the message is written out along the each side.

For example, the message "The Scytale was used in Ancient Greece" written on an 8-sided Scytale would look like this:

T H E S
C Y T A
L E W A
S U S E
D I N A
N C I E
N T G R
E E C E

When the strap is unwound, the plain-text characters are tranposed into one long column of characters like this: TCLSDNNEHYEUICTEETWSNIGCSAAEAERE

To recover the original message, the receiver wraps the strap around a Scytale of the same dimensions and size as the one used to write the message, and reads the plain-text off each side in turn.
